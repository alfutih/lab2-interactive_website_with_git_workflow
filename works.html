Playrent

Playrent is an app that allows musicians to rent, share, and exchange instruments instead of buying new ones.
I designed the user flow, user interface, and visual style to make the experience simple, intuitive, and playful for users.

Challenges:

Understanding musicians’ needs, creating a smooth rental process, and balancing a fun visual identity with usability and clarity.

An Honest Interfaces

The project explores how car interfaces can reveal the hidden environmental and ethical impacts of driving.
Through critical design, I created visual concepts that make
the consequences of user actions visible and emotionally engaging.

Challenges:

Translating abstract ethical ideas into clear visuals, maintaining a balance between critique and usability,
and designing interactions that encourage reflection instead of frustration.

Touch & Discover

An interactive museum console designed to enhance the visitor experience by bringing the story of legendary Swedish boxer Ingemar Johansson to life.
This touchscreen-based interface allows users to explore various categories — including competition highlights, personal stories, photographs, and awards — using an intuitive layout.

Challenges:

Designing intuitive touch interactions that supports the surrounding exhibits.

Skräphjältarna

An educational children’s book aimed at teaching children about recycling.
I drew the whole book, including all characters, to make sustainability fun and engaging for younger audiences.

Challenges:

Drawing characters and pages that fit the text’s concept and ideas, and illustrating the book in a child-friendly style.
